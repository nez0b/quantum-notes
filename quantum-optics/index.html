Quantum Optics

From foundational physics to the optical SPU research program.

A self-contained track that builds from coherent states and cavity QED up through the Coherent Ising Machine and the in-progress optical Stochastic Processing Unit (SPU) proposal. Notes 01–05 are foundational; Note 06 is the headline applied note (CIM); Note 07 ties everything to the live research project at cim-spu-optical-simulation. Source PDFs are in _lectures/.

  • Foundations

  • 01.Coherent States & the Quadrature Picture

    Field quantization, vacuum fluctuations, displacement operator, squeezed light, Wigner functions.

  • 02.Linear Optics: Beam Splitters & Networks

    Two-mode mixing, Mach-Zehnder, Reck/Clements decomposition, photonic meshes.

  • 03.Nonlinear Optics & Parametric Processes

    χ⁽²⁾ and χ⁽³⁾ susceptibilities, three-wave mixing, parametric down-conversion, squeezing generation.

  • 04.Cavity QED & Open Quantum Systems

    Driven cavities, Lindblad master equation, input-output formalism, the bridge to classical Langevin.

  • 05.Homodyne & Continuous Measurement

    Balanced homodyne, shot noise, sub-shot-noise sensing, Wiseman-Milburn measurement-feedback.

  • Applied — the CIM and beyond

  • 06.OPO, DOPO, and the Coherent Ising Machine

    From parametric oscillation to MFB-CIM hardware. When is the machine quantum, when classical, and what does the bifurcation actually do?

  • 07.Thermodynamic LA & the OU Machine Proposal

    Langevin → Lyapunov, the optical SPU adaptation, hardware envelope, and the bridge to the underdamped √κ research direction.
